Finding the Name

"Be your energy. Together. Or alone. And it's wonderful."

स्पन्द

Spanda

Sanskrit — "the divine creative pulse"

The subtle vibration of consciousness as it manifests into living form. Not a gross movement — a tremor. The heartbeat before creation. Music is vibration. Fractals are patterns from pulsation. Evolution is creative pulse. The word literally means "to move a little" — the smallest motion that creates everything.

"This divine power is Spanda — the vibration of consciousness. The creative impulse that makes the formless take form."
— Kashmir Śaivism, 9th century

/|\

Awen

Welsh — "flowing spirit"

From the Indo-European root meaning "to blow." Same root as awel — breeze, wind. The inspiration of the poets. The breath of the gods. The song of nature itself. You're literally playing as wind carrying flame petals through evolving worlds. The ancient druidic symbol /|\ represents three rays of light converging.

"Aw" means flowing. "Wen" means spirit, or a being. Together: a flowing being. A spirit in motion.

रस

Rasa

Sanskrit — "essence, taste, emotional flavor of art"

In Indian aesthetics: the emotional juice that art evokes in a sensitive viewer — a sahṛdaya, literally "one who has heart." There are nine rasas: love, wonder, courage, peace, humor, sadness, fury, fear, and disgust. Your game literally has a taste model that learns what beauty means to each player. The word is 4 letters. Easy to say in any language. Deeply meaningful.

"The aesthetic flavour that evokes an indescribable feeling — relished by one who has heart."
— Natya Shastra, ~200 BCE

幽玄

Yūgen

Japanese — "profound, mysterious beauty"

An awareness of the universe that triggers emotional responses too deep for words. The half-seen moon behind drifting clouds. The distant toll of a bell at dusk. Beauty that suggests more than it reveals — which is exactly what fractal flames do. Yū means "dim, mysterious." Gen means "profound, hidden."

"It is not an allusion to another world. It is about this world, this experience."

सहज

Sahaja

Sanskrit — "born together, the natural state of being"

Literally "co-emergent" — things arising together naturally. The spontaneous state. Being effortlessly yourself. No trying, no striving, just being. Together or alone, it doesn't matter — you are in your natural state. This is the word for "just be... be your energy."

"Saha" — together. "Ja" — born. Born together. Naturally. As you are.

संगम

Sangam

Sanskrit — "confluence, where rivers meet"

The meeting place of rivers. Where different streams merge into one. Players' flames meeting and cross-pollinating. Music meeting visuals. Genomes converging. Strangers sharing a journey. The most sacred Sangam is where three rivers — the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati — converge.

Where everything flows together.

My Heart Says: Spanda

It captures the most of what you described. The creative pulse. The vibration that IS music. The subtle tremor that makes fractals bloom. The shared consciousness of players drifting together. It's gentle. It's warm. It's ancient (9th century Kashmir). It's unused in games. And it sounds like what it means — a soft pulse. Spanda.

But honestly? Awen is a close second. "Flowing spirit" with the wind etymology is almost too perfect for a game about flying through flames. And it's beautiful to say.

These can also be starting points — we could riff on them, combine roots, invent something new.
